Infrared, although it carries ~49% of solar energy, plays a relatively small role in ocean heating because it is absorbed so quickly. IR warms only the ocean skin layer, and the heat is immediately re-radiated or lost to evaporation. Visible light—still a large fraction of sunlight—heats water down to tens of meters and is primarily responsible for warming the bulk of the upper ocean.
