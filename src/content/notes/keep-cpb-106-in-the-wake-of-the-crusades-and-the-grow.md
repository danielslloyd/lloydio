In the wake of the Crusades and the growing influence of the Church, knighthood was reformed - codes of chivalry began replacing violent and thuggish behavior. Priests began administering knighting ceremonies rather than other knights, and knights (previously a class of upwardly mobile warriors) became more closely entwined with the nobility.
