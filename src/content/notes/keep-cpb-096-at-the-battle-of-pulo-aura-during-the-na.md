At the Battle of Pulo Aura during the Napoleonic Wars, Commodore Nathaniel Dance commanded a large convoy of unescorted EIC merchant ships that was surprised by a powerful French naval squadron. He arranged his ships in a line of battle, bluffing that they were in fact ships of the line. When the French force retreated, Dance pursued them to keep up the charade until his ships were out of danger. His convoy arrived safely with an intact cargo of tea, silk and porcelain worth over £8 million, and Dance was knighted.
