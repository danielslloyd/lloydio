At the outset of the Seven Years' War, Britain sent three expeditions: Braddock, a naval expedition to intercept French reinforcements, and a New England militia expedition to Nova Scotia. The naval expedition was partially successful and the militia took Nova Scotia, but Braddock was utterly defeated. The upshot was that Britain appeared to be the aggressor in the Seven Years War but had not achieved a decisive victory. Thus France was not discouraged from entering the war.
