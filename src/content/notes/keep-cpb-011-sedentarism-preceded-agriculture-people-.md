Sedentarism preceded agriculture — people settled into permanent or semi-permanent communities before they began cultivating crops, inverting the intuitive assumption that farming drove people to settle down. The archaeological record suggests that abundance of wild resources, not farming, first enabled and incentivized fixed settlement.
