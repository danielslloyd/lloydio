Specie scarcity in 18th-century Massachusetts led first to taxes being paid in-kind with agricultural commodities, then to the creation of the Land Bank, which issued paper notes backed by mortgages on the land of the holders. Hundreds of people subscribed - among them Samuel Adams Sr. - and were ruined when the bank was dissolved by an act of parliament.
