The Spanish Civil War started as a military coup against the newly established Second Spanish Republic. The coup was partially successful, setting the stage for a civil war. Germany and Italy quickly aligned with the Nationalists, while western European democracies (fearful of widening the conflict) enforced a neutrality agreement that effectively embargoed Republican Spain. Anarchists and Communists joined the Republicans in an uneasy coalition, while foreign volunteers (relatively few in number, but widely publicized) flocked to join.
