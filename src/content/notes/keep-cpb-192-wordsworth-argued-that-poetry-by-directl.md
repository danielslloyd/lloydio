Wordsworth argued that poetry, by directly imparting emotions from author to reader, was superior to prose, and that the objective of such poetry was to connect readers with the long-forgotten reality of their authentic emotions. He often chose rural subjects and themes, contrasting them with artificial, mechanical, prosaic city life.
