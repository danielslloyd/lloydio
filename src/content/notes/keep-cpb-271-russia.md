Russian rulers traditionally had two aspects of leadership that were expected from a Czar: their grozny (power and might) and tshishie (caring and nurturing)

Traditional Marxist dogma held that capitalism was a necessary historical step before communism and would eventually collapse, but some revolutionaries believed they could "skip past" capitalism straight to communism

The Russian Social Democratic Labour Party (RSDLP) splintered at its 2nd Congress in 1903 following a dispute between Julius Martov and Vladimir Lenin. Martov and his supporters viewed freedom and democracy as internally good natural rights, and favored educating the populace to foment change. Lenin, on the other hand, saw no value in freedom or rights beyond their immediate utilitarian value, and believed that a revolutionary vanguard of hardcore believers was necessary. Accordingly, Martov's faction favored a more expansive definition of party membership, while Lenin wanted to restrict it to a dedicated few. Lenin won a crucial vote on the question of party membership, leading to his faction taking the name Bolsheviks, from большинство (majority) and Martov's faction being called Mensheviks, from меньшинство (minority).
