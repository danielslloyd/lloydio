The Articles of Confederation were drafted shortly after the Declaration of Independence but were not approved by all states until much later, in part because Maryland and other states objected to the territorial claims of the colonies carrying over to the United States. Large territorial claims were a significant source of revenue for the states, as commonly-held land was sold off to individual landowners.
