Japanese shipboard anti-aircraft fire was very ineffective in WW2 - their doctrine relied on maneuver, and the primitive fire computers of the time could not recompute quickly enough to keep up with changing headings. Of the roughly 150 US aircraft lost at Midway, only 2 were confirmed AA kills.
