The human airway is coated in a mucus that traps foreign particles before they reach the delicate membranes of the lungs. This mucus is constantly being replenished, and the old mucus is pushed up into the throat by cilia. Nicotine paralyzes the cilia, causing the mucus to become saturated and less effective. Immediately after smokers quit, their newly-active cilia begin pushing up the saturated mucus.
