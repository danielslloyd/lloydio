To reclaim land from the sea, the Dutch had to build a dyke, pump out the sea water, then "rinse" the alluvial soil left behind by pumping in fresh water and draining repeatedly to get rid of the excess salt. This constant pumping was aided greatly by windmills, particularly after the advent of rotating windmills that could pump regardless of wind direction
