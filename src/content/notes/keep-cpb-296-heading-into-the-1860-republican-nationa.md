Heading into the 1860 Republican National Convention, Lincoln was not considered a likely candidate. Chicago was selected as the convention site in part because it was considered neutral ground between the likely candidates: William Seward (later Secretary of State), Salmon Chase (later Treasury secretary), and Edwin Bates (later Attorney General). Lincoln was chosen largely as the consensus second choice of multiple competing factions.
