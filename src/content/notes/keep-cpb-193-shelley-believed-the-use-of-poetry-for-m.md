Shelley believed the use of poetry for moral refinement in its readers. By directly engaging emotion and imagination rather than reason, poetry can cultivate empathy and thus, ethical improvement. In describing their role in shaping cultural morality through aesthetics, Shelley called poets "the unacknowledged legislators of the World".
