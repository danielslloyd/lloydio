The U.S. freight rail system is much more dominant than Europe's, moving much more cargo per capita at a much lower cost. This is because privately-owned railways optimized for profit with bulk commodity movement across continental distances where rail has no cost-effective competitor. State-owned European railways were optimized instead for politically visible passenger service across shorter, denser corridors where high fuel taxes and city-center-to-city-center travel times made rail competitive with both cars and planes.
