British presence in India was confined to trading posts until the mid 1770s, when adventurers in the employ of the East India Company (notably Robert Clive) exploited European military supremacy to play kingmakers in various territories of the crumbling Mogul empire. Each successive client granted lucrative concessions (monetary and territorial) to the EIC upon taking power. Warren Hastings (Governor-General of Bengal from 1772–1785) was opposed to alliances and conquests in India but prosecuted a successful war on the French and their Marathi and Mysorean allies, the financing of which played a major role in his impeachment trial.
