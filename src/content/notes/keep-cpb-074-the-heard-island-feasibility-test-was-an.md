The Heard Island Feasibility Test was an experiment in 1991 that transmitted very low frequency sound from a point in the southern Indian Ocean that had a "line of sight" to both coasts of the US. A network of listening stations was able to use precise measurements of the speed of sound to estimate the water temperature. The experiment is was stopped due to equipment failure and concerns about marine mammals, which were poorly understood at the time.
