After Sumter, a few slave states (including Virginia) seemed poised to remain in the Union, but Lincoln issued a strong call to arms that convinced many Southerners that he was ready to invade, not merely to hold Federal forts. Many contemporaries blamed this order for prompting Virginia's secession.
