At the end of the Younger Dryas (~9600 BC), Greenland temperatures rose roughly 10°C within a decade or two. This event marks one of the sharpest climate transitions in the ice core record and is widely regarded as the climatic catalyst that made sedentary agriculture suddenly viable across much of the world.
