Andrew Jackson was intensely patriotic (opposing nullification and hints of secession) and identified himself and his agenda with the will of the people. He significantly expanded executive power through use of the veto and personal domination of his cabinet. His opponents denounced this as monarchical and formed the Whig party, named for the British faction that opposed absolute monarchy.
