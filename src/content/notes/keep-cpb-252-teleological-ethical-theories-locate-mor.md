teleological ethical theories locate moral goodness in the consequences of our actions.... There are many philosophers who reject the entire teleological agenda by arguing that moral goodness has nothing to do with the pleasure, happiness, and or consequences. Deontological theories are by definition duty-based. That is to say, that morality, according to deontology, consists in the fulfillment of moral obligations, or duties. Duties, in the deontological tradition, are most often associated with obeying absolute moral rules. TELEOLOGICAL AND DEONTOLOGICAL THEORIES By: Ronald F. White, Ph.D.
